System Architecture

The Third Conditional

Used to talk about unreal situations in the past and their imaginary past results. Often used for regrets or criticism.

Structure:
If + Past Perfect, would have + Past Participle

"If she had known the code, she would have stopped the hack."

(Reality: She didn't know the code, so she didn't stop the hack).

"They wouldn't have died if they had stayed in the ship."

System Architecture

Mixed Conditionals

Used to talk about an unreal past action that has an unreal present result.

Structure (Type 3 + 2):
If + Past Perfect, would + verb (base form)

"If the timeline hadn't split, we would be safe now."

(Reality: The timeline split in the past, so we are not safe now).

"I wouldn't be so tired if I had slept last night."
